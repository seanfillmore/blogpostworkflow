## Ingredient Accuracy
VERDICT: Mostly accurate — one meaningful flag
NOTES: The post correctly identifies and highlights the brand's actual formula ingredients: organic virgin coconut oil, organic beeswax, organic red palm oil, organic vanilla extract, and organic citrus essential oils. These are all named correctly and described accurately in the "Why Real Skin Care" section. The flag: **shea butter and jojoba oil** are both presented in the "Best Ingredients to Look For" section with meaningful positive framing — shea butter gets called "excellent for very dry or chapped lips" and jojoba is described as "absorbs well and doesn't feel greasy." Neither ingredient appears in Real Skin Care's formula. Recommending ingredients the brand doesn't use is a recurring pattern flagged in standing feedback, and while these appear in a general guide section rather than a product description, readers moving from that guidance to the product CTA may reasonably expect those ingredients to be present. Consider either adding a brief clarification ("RSC's formula takes a different approach, using red palm oil for similar richness") or softening the framing so it doesn't read as an implicit product promise. This is not a blocker-level error since the actual ingredient list is accurately disclosed in the brand section, but it should be addressed. Castor oil, vitamin E, and candelilla wax face the same issue but with less prominent framing. Product format (lip balm tube) is correctly identified throughout — no stick/bar/cream confusion.
