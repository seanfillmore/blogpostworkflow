2. David’s Natural Toothpaste – Peppermint Power and Sleek Packaging

David’s Natural Toothpaste is popular for its strong flavor and aluminum tube. It’s SLS-free, fluoride-free, and uses stevia and xylitol as sweeteners. While it’s more foamy and bold than Dr. Bronner’s, it may be too intense for users who prefer a more subtle profile.

3. Redmond Earthpaste – Simple, Salty, and Clay-Based

Redmond Earthpaste skips all the extras: no glycerin, no foaming agents, and no synthetic flavors. It uses bentonite clay and sea salt for its cleaning action. If you like Dr. Bronner’s minimalist ingredient list but want an even more stripped-down experience, Earthpaste could be for you — though the texture takes getting used to.

4. Risewell Mineral Toothpaste – Modern and Dentist-Backed

Risewell includes hydroxyapatite for enamel support and a lightly foaming texture. It’s free from fluoride and SLS, but the ingredient list is longer than most natural brands. Some may prefer to skip lab-derived alternatives in favor of whole ingredients like those found in Real Skin Care’s version.

5. Tom’s of Maine Botanically Bright – Accessible with Caveats

Tom’s offers natural options that are widely available in stores. The Botanically Bright formula features peppermint and stevia, along with a blend of botanical extracts. However, the presence of glycerin and natural flavors may not work for ingredient purists.

6. Jack N’ Jill Natural Toothpaste – Mild and Great for Kids

Jack N’ Jill is a top choice for kids and sensitive users who find even Dr. Bronner’s too strong. It’s fluoride-free, SLS-free, and flavor-forward without synthetic ingredients. While not as robust in performance, it’s a safe pick for daily use across all ages.
