2. Dr. Bronner’s All-One Toothpaste – Organic with a Herbal Touch

Dr. Bronner’s offers a fluoride-free toothpaste made with certified organic ingredients. It’s low-foam, naturally flavored, and clean — though the runny texture and herbal flavor profile don’t appeal to everyone. Still, it’s a great pick for ingredient-conscious shoppers who want a gentler option.

3. Redmond Earthpaste – The Minimalist’s Dream

Earthpaste uses just a handful of ingredients, including bentonite clay, water, sea salt, and essential oils. There’s no glycerin, fluoride, or sweeteners of any kind. It’s about as clean as you can get, but its earthy taste and texture are very different from traditional pastes — which can be a dealbreaker for some.

4. Tom’s of Maine Children’s Toothpaste – Mild, Simple, and Easy to Find

For those seeking a mild option, Tom’s children’s toothpaste (yes, even for adults!) offers a safe, non-irritating formula. It’s free of fluoride and artificial flavors, though it does contain glycerin and xylitol. A good transition toothpaste for those with sensitivity to strong flavors like Davids.

5. Hello SLS-Free Toothpaste – Trendy and Accessible

Hello’s fluoride-free options come in fun flavors and sleek packaging, appealing to a younger audience. It avoids SLS and dyes, but still includes sweeteners like erythritol and stevia, which can cause irritation in some users — especially when used multiple times daily.

6. Risewell Toothpaste – Science-Backed, But Not for Everyone

Risewell includes hydroxyapatite and a full suite of natural ingredients to promote enamel strength and oral health. A good choice if you want performance, but not if you’re going ultra-clean.
