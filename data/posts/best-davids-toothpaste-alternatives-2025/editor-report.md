## 5. FACTUAL CONCERNS
**VERDICT:** Needs Work — FLAG  
**NOTES:** Three claims require tightening:

1. **"Coconut oil is known for its antibacterial properties"** — This is a broad claim without citation. While coconut oil does have some antimicrobial compounds (lauric acid), presenting it as a standalone oral-health benefit without nuance could be misleading. Consider: "Coconut oil contains lauric acid, which some research suggests may support oral health" or add a source.

2. **"This toothpaste supports oral health by nourishing the mouth's natural microbiome"** — This is aspirational language. Real Skin Care's toothpaste has not been clinically proven to "nourish the microbiome." Safer claim: "Made without harsh chemicals that can disrupt your mouth's natural balance" (avoidance framing is defensible; active support is overreach).

3. **"Hello SLS-Free Toothpaste…sweeteners like erythritol and stevia, which can cause irritation in some users — especially when used multiple times daily"** — This is presented as fact but lacks evidence. Erythritol and stevia irritation is not well-established in peer-reviewed literature. Either cite a source or soften to: "Some users report sensitivity to sweeteners; individual tolerance varies."

Per Writer Standing Rules (added 2026-04-08), factual concerns have been flagged 21 of 30 days. Tighten before submission.
