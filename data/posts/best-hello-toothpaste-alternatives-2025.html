2. David’s Natural Toothpaste – Premium, Polished, and Plastic-Free

David’s Natural Toothpaste has earned a loyal following thanks to its sleek aluminum packaging and whitening claims. Made in the USA with natural ingredients and available in a variety of flavors, David’s is fluoride-free and SLS-free. However, it does include stevia and some essential oils that can be too intense for sensitive users.

3. Risewell Mineral Toothpaste – Dentist-Approved with Hydroxyapatite

Risewell uses hydroxyapatite, a naturally occurring mineral that strengthens enamel. This fluoride-free, sulfate-free formula is backed by dentists, but it comes with a higher price point and a more complex ingredient list than other clean brands.

4. Dr. Bronner’s All-One Toothpaste – Organic and Refreshing

Dr. Bronner’s All-One Toothpaste features organic coconut oil, aloe, and silica for a mild clean. It’s free from synthetic foaming agents, though some may find the texture thin and the flavor subtle compared to conventional options.

5. Tom’s of Maine SLS-Free Toothpaste – Easy to Find, Clean Enough

Tom’s of Maine is one of the most widely available natural brands, offering several SLS-free and fluoride-free options. While generally gentle, some varieties still contain carrageenan or sodium lauryl sulfate — so checking labels is essential.

6. Redmond Earthpaste – Clay-Based with Minimal Ingredients

Redmond Earthpaste is ideal for ingredient purists. Its bentonite clay, sea salt, and essential oils formula skips foaming agents and sweeteners entirely. The texture is unique, but its simplicity appeals to those avoiding additives.

Why Switch from Hello Toothpaste?

Hello positions itself as a clean brand, yet many formulas include erythritol, SLS alternatives, and artificial flavors. For people with sensitive mouths or a focus on ingredient purity, the alternatives above — especially Real Skin Care — offer a more transparent, minimal approach to oral care.
