What Causes Tooth Sensitivity?

Tooth sensitivity usually happens when the protective enamel wears down or gums recede, exposing the underlying dentin. This layer contains tiny tubes that connect to nerve endings, which can trigger pain when exposed to hot, cold, or sweet foods. Common causes include:

  • Aggressive brushing or hard-bristled toothbrushes
  • Teeth grinding (bruxism)
  • Acidic foods or drinks
  • Gum recession or gum disease
  • Whitening treatments

What to Look For in Toothpaste for Sensitive Teeth

  • SLS-Free Formula: Sodium lauryl sulfate can irritate sensitive gums and worsen discomfort.
  • No Harsh Abrasives: Gentle polishing agents prevent additional enamel wear.
  • Alcohol-Free: Avoids the burning sensation that often accompanies conventional pastes.
  • Fluoride-Free (Optional): Some people with sensitivity prefer natural remineralizing alternatives like baking soda or hydroxyapatite.
  • Soothing Ingredients: Coconut oil, aloe vera, and xylitol may help reduce inflammation and bacterial buildup.

Who Needs Toothpaste for Sensitive Teeth?

While sensitivity can affect anyone, you're especially at risk if:

  • You’ve had recent dental work or whitening treatments
  • You experience pain when eating hot or cold foods
  • You use whitening or tartar-control toothpastes
  • You brush vigorously or use a medium/firm toothbrush

How to Use Sensitive Toothpaste Effectively

  • Brush Gently Twice a Day: Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and avoid applying too much pressure.
  • Don’t Rinse Immediately: Let the ingredients remain on your teeth for maximum effect.
  • Stay Consistent: Relief builds with continued use over days or weeks.
  • Avoid Acidic Foods: Citrus and soda can worsen enamel erosion.
