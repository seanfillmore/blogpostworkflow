Frequently Asked Questions

Is “unscented” the same as fragrance-free?
No. “Unscented” products can include masking agents to cover natural odors. “Fragrance-free” means no fragrance chemicals (synthetic or natural) are added.

Why avoid fragrance in body lotion?
Fragrance is a common irritant and can trigger allergies or sensitivity—especially on compromised or reactive skin. Going fragrance-free reduces the risk of redness, itching, and flare-ups.

Are essential oils okay if I’m sensitive to fragrance?
Not always. Even natural essential oils contain allergens that can irritate sensitive skin. If you’re highly reactive, choose products with no added fragrance or essential oils.

Can fragrance-free lotions be used during pregnancy?
Many people prefer fragrance-free during pregnancy to minimize exposure to potential irritants. Always review ingredient lists and consult your healthcare provider if you have concerns.

What makes Real Skin Care’s lotion different?

How should I patch test a new lotion?
Apply a small amount to the inner forearm or behind the knee once daily for 2–3 days. If no redness or itching occurs, begin regular use.

Is it safe for babies and kids?
Yes. The minimal, fragrance-free formula is designed to be gentle enough for the whole family. Avoid broken skin and consult a pediatrician for specific concerns.

Will a fragrance-free lotion still have a smell?
You may notice a faint natural aroma from the base ingredients (like oils or waxes). That’s normal and not the same as added fragrance.
