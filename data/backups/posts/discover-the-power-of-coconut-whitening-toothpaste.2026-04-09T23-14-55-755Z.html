Kids & Sensitive Gums

If you’re shopping for toothpaste for sensitive gums or for kids, look for low-foam, clean formulas without SLS or artificial flavors. Use a pea-size amount, brush gently with a soft bristle, and remind children to spit and rinse. If your gums are prone to sensitivity, reduce pressure and consider shorter, more frequent brushes while your mouth adjusts to a low-foam, non-irritating routine.

How to Use for Best Results

  1. Portion: Use a pea-size amount (children and adults). A little goes a long way.
  2. Brush: Clean for two minutes with a soft-bristle or electric brush, reaching all surfaces.
  3. Rinse: Spit and rinse thoroughly. Do not swallow.
  4. Consistency: Brush twice daily and replace your brush head every 3 months.

Sensitivity note: If you’re reactive to baking soda, reduce frequency or patch test along the gumline; discontinue use if irritation occurs.

Transition Tips: Switching from Conventional Paste

  • Expect low foam: Without SLS, foam is lighter — the clean is still thorough when you brush for two minutes.
  • Flavor shift: Essential-oil mint tastes cleaner and rinses without an artificial aftertaste.
  • Give it a week: If you’re used to heavy foam, the first few days can feel different. Most people prefer the gentler feel after a short adjustment.
  • Baking soda benefits: Gentle polishing helps with surface stains; if you’re soda-sensitive, use less and monitor comfort.
