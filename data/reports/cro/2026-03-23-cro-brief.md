## Summary

The store is in a critical state: **zero orders and zero revenue across all 6 days** despite receiving 25–50 daily sessions in Clarity and meaningful organic impressions (up to 909 on a single blog page). The site is generating content-driven organic visibility but completely failing to convert visitors into buyers. All Google Ads campaigns are paused, pages per session hovers near 1.0, scroll depth averages only ~43%, and visitors are bouncing from blog content without ever reaching product pages. The #1 priority is fixing the content-to-commerce bridge.

## Action Items

### 1. Add Prominent CTAs and Product Links to Top Blog Posts — HIGH
**Evidence:** The "coconut oil as toothpaste" blog got 333 impressions and 3 clicks (GSC, Mar 10); "best coconut oil body lotions" got 111 impressions (Mar 20); "fluoride free toothpaste" got 909 impressions (Mar 19) — yet pages per session is 1.0–1.15 and **0 conversions across 6 days**. Visitors land on blog content and leave without visiting a product page.
**Action:** On every top-traffic blog post, insert an in-content product card (image + price + "Shop Now" button) within the first 300 words and again at the midpoint. Use sticky mobile CTA bars on blog pages linking to the relevant product/collection. Prioritize: "can you use coconut oil as toothpaste," "best coconut oil body lotions," "best fluoride free toothpaste," "toxic chemicals in soap," and "coconut oil deodorant."

### 2. Fix 404 Errors Losing Real Visitors — HIGH
**Evidence:** "404 Not Found" appeared as a top page in Clarity on Mar 22 with 3 sessions (6% of daily traffic). With only ~30–50 real sessions/day, losing even 3 to broken pages is significant.
**Action:** Check Shopify's 404 log and Clarity session recordings for the broken URLs. Set up 301 redirects to the most relevant live pages. Install a URL redirect app (e.g., Easy Redirects) and audit all internal links in blog posts for dead links.

### 3. Improve Scroll Depth and On-Page Engagement — HIGH
**Evidence:** Average scroll depth is 35–48% across the 6-day period; active time averages only 25–53 seconds; script errors affect 10–19% of sessions consistently.
**Action:** (a) Investigate and fix the JavaScript errors appearing in 10–19% of sessions — check Clarity's error recordings to identify the scripts. Broken JS may be hiding CTAs or blocking add-to-cart functionality. (b) Restructure blog posts to front-load key takeaways, product recommendations, and comparison tables above the 40% scroll mark. (c) Add a sticky "Shop Our Products" nav bar that persists on scroll.

### 4. Reactivate at Least One Google Ads Campaign with a Small Test Budget — MED
**Evidence:** All 15 Google Ads campaigns are paused/removed, resulting in $0 spend and 0 paid traffic. Organic alone is delivering only 1–3 clicks/day from GSC, and GA4 shows only 0–26 sessions/day (mostly direct). The store has no paid acquisition engine running.
**Action:** Re-enable the "Non-Toxic Coconut Lotion" or "Body Lotion - Search" campaign at $10–15/day targeting high-intent keywords like "non toxic body lotion," "coconut oil body lotion buy," and "natural body lotion for dry skin." Set up proper conversion tracking in GA4 before reactivating. These collection pages already get organic impressions (97 for organic-body-lotion, 129 for vegan-body-lotion on a single day).

### 5. Optimize Title Tags and Meta Descriptions for High-Impression, Low-CTR Pages — MED
**Evidence:** "best-coconut-oil-body-lotions" blog: 111 impressions, avg position 4.8, but only 0.9% CTR (Mar 20). "coconut-oil-deodorant" blog: 252 impressions, position 5.4, but 0.4% CTR. "can-you-use-coconut-oil-as-toothpaste": 250 impressions, position 3.8, 0.4% CTR. These pages rank well but titles/descriptions aren't compelling enough to earn clicks.
**Action:** Rewrite title tags to include the current year (2026), a benefit hook, and the primary keyword. Example: "Can You Use Coconut Oil as Toothpaste? (2026 Guide + Our #1 Pick)" instead of the current generic title. Add star ratings or price schema markup to make product-related listings stand out in SERPs.

### 6. Reduce Bot Traffic Polluting Analytics — LOW
**Evidence:** Clarity shows bot counts of 33–665/day vs. real sessions of only 7–50 (and several days show negative "real" values, indicating misclassification). On Mar 22–23, bots = 665 while total sessions = 36–50, creating unreliable data.
**Action:** Add bot filtering in Clarity settings. In Shopify, install a bot-blocking app or configure Cloudflare firewall rules to block known bot user agents. This won't directly drive revenue but will ensure your analytics are trustworthy for future CRO decisions.

### 7. Create a Homepage Conversion Path — MED
**Evidence:** The homepage ("/") was the top GA4 landing page on Mar 18 with 10 of 26 sessions, and on Mar 19 with 2 of 7 sessions. In GSC, the homepage gets 52–116 impressions/day. Yet 0 conversions occurred. Clarity shows the homepage consistently in the top 2 most-visited pages.
**Action:** Add a hero section with a clear value prop ("Coconut Oil Skincare — 6 Ingredients or Less"), a bestseller product carousel with prices and "Add to Cart" buttons visible above the fold, and a limited-time offer (free shipping threshold or first-order discount) with urgency language. Currently visitors hit the homepage and leave without engaging further.
