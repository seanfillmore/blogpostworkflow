# Real Skin Care: Backlink Gap Analysis & Outreach Strategy

## How to Read This Report

All links are **nofollow**, which limits direct SEO equity transfer — but these placements still drive **referral traffic, brand discovery, and topical authority signals**. Priority should be weighted toward high-DR editorial and press domains first.

## Category 1: Editorial / Blog Reviews

*Independent bloggers or content sites that have reviewed or recommended competitor products organically.*

| Domain | DR | Why It Likely Links to Competitors |
|---|---|---|
| everydayroots.com referrers → **huffpost.com** | 92 | Staff or contributor articles on natural/DIY beauty routines |
| **sheknows.com** | 82 | Lifestyle editorial: natural beauty swaps, ingredient spotlights |
| **brit.co** | 81 | DIY/wellness content reviewing clean personal care products |
| **domino.com** | 80 | Home + lifestyle editorial; clean bathroom/beauty shelf features |
| **bustle.com** | 88 | Staff-written product recommendation articles ("best natural deodorants") |

**Outreach Angle:**

> Pitch a **free product sampling package** to the beauty/wellness editor alongside a one-page brand brief that leads with a differentiator competitors can't match. For Real Skin Care, that hook is: *"All products are formulated without [specific flagged ingredient — e.g., baking soda, synthetic fragrance] AND are available as a complete routine — deodorant, toothpaste, lotion, lip balm, bar soap — from a single brand."* Most competitors they already cover are single-SKU brands (PiperWai = deodorant only, Native = deodorant-first). Position Real Skin Care as the **one-stop natural bathroom routine** — a fresh angle editors haven't already covered.

| **fabfitfun.com** | 74 | Curated product recommendation lists tied to subscription box features |
| **milled.com** | 74 | Email newsletter aggregator — likely indexes competitor promotional emails as product discovery |

**Outreach Angle:**

> For roundup editors, **don't pitch the brand — pitch the gap in their existing article.** Research the specific URL that links to competitors (use Ahrefs/SimilarWeb to find the exact page). Then email: *"Your [article title] roundup mentions Native and Schmidt's but doesn't include a brand that covers [specific angle — e.g., aluminum-free deodorant + fluoride-free toothpaste in one line]. Readers who want a full natural routine currently have no single recommendation. Real Skin Care fills that gap — happy to send samples or provide product specs for your next update."* This works especially well for Refinery29 and Nylon, whose roundups are updated seasonally.

## Category 3: Press & Media

*News outlets, major publications, and content platforms with editorial standards and staff writers.*

| Domain | DR | Why It Likely Links to Competitors |
|---|---|---|
| **forbes.com** | 94 | Business/entrepreneurship coverage of indie beauty brands; "clean beauty market" features |
| **squarespace.com** | 95 | Customer showcase/case studies — competitors likely built sites on Squarespace |
| **prnewswire.com** | 92 | Press releases distributed by competitors (Native, PiperWai, Schmidt's had funded rounds/launches) |
| **substack.com** | 93 | Independent beauty/wellness journalists with paid newsletters recommending products |
| **youtube.com** | 99 | Influencer/creator product reviews; competitors gifted product or ran affiliate programs |

**Outreach Angle:**

> - **Forbes:** Pitch the founder's story or a business angle — *"From [founding story] to competing with P&G-owned Schmidt's without VC funding"* — to a contributor covering indie CPG or clean beauty market trends. Forbes contributor network is open; find one who covers beauty or wellness entrepreneurship.
> - **PRNewswire:** Write and distribute a press release tied to a genuine news hook: new product launch, retail partnership, sustainability certification, or a study/survey about consumer attitudes toward natural personal care. This is a **direct buy** — no outreach needed.
> - **Substack:** Identify 5–10 newsletters in the clean beauty, wellness, or natural living niche. Offer a **dedicated discount code + affiliate commission** for their readers. Substack links are nofollow but newsletter audiences have high purchase intent.
> - **YouTube:** Build a seeding list of creators in the 10K–200K subscriber range reviewing natural deodorant or clean beauty. Send a **full routine kit** (deodorant + toothpaste + lotion + soap) — the variety gives creators more content than a single-product competitor send.
> - **Squarespace:** This is likely a template showcase or customer story link — only worth pursuing if Real Skin Care's site is built on Squarespace. If so, apply directly to their [customer stories program](https://www.squarespace.com/customer-stories).
