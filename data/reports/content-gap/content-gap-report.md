## 3. Buyer Journey Analysis

### Top of Funnel (TOF) — Awareness: **Severely Underserved**

RSC has almost no content targeting people who don't yet know what they're looking for. Competitors like Tom's of Maine capture massive TOF audiences with purely educational articles:

- "oily hair" (874 traffic/mo) — no product pitch, pure information
- "cinnamon and honey benefits" (826 traffic/mo) — lifestyle wellness content
- "home remedies for oral thrush in babies" (737 traffic/mo) — problem-aware, not solution-aware
- "natural makeup remover" (583 traffic/mo) — adjacent lifestyle topic
- OSEA drives 677 visits/mo from "how to use hyaluronic acid" — pure education

RSC's closest TOF content includes "11 Tea Tree Oil Benefits for Skin and Daily Use," "What Is Natural Skincare? Benefits & Tips," and "Coconut Oil Pulling: Benefits for Oral Health" — but these are isolated. The brand has no systematic strategy for capturing informational searches at scale. Keywords like "sls toothpaste" (1,600/mo, KD 4, RSC at position 90), "no fluoride toothpaste" (1,200/mo, KD 8, RSC at position 100), and "organic coconut oil for skin" (800/mo, KD 12, RSC at position 99) are pure TOF opportunities where RSC is effectively invisible.

### Middle of Funnel (MOF) — Consideration: **Partially Covered, Execution Gaps**

RSC has invested heavily in comparison and roundup content (MOF is the strongest stage):

- 12+ "Best [X] Toothpaste" posts
- 5+ "Best [X] Body Lotion" posts
- 5+ "Best [X] Deodorant" posts
- Multiple brand-alternative roundups (Boka, Davids, Tom's, Hello, Risewell, Dr. Bronner's)

**The problem:** Execution is inconsistent. RSC has a "Best Fluoride Free Toothpaste" post but ranks at position 76 for "best fluoride free toothpaste" (1,700/mo, KD 0). It has a "Best SLS Free Toothpaste" post but ranks #16 for the highest-volume variant. Entire product categories — lip balm and bar soap — have zero MOF content despite RSC selling both. "Natural lip balm" (4,900/mo, KD 6) and "natural bar soap for men" (700/mo, KD 1) have no RSC content at all.

### Bottom of Funnel (BOF) — Conversion: **Over-Indexed Relative to TOF**

RSC's BOF content (product-specific pages, alternative roundups) is proportionally the most developed, yet the brand lacks the TOF funnel feeding into it. Posts like "Best Davids Toothpaste Alternatives," "Best Risewell Alternatives," and "Best Tom's of Maine Alternatives" are pure BOF — they target people already in buying mode. These are valuable but only work if enough users enter the funnel at earlier stages.

Tom's of Maine's highest-traffic content has almost nothing to do with direct product promotion. Their top pages include:

- "how to make rosemary essential oil" (910 traffic/mo, 116 keywords)
- "oily hair" (874 traffic/mo, 114 keywords)
- "toothpaste flavors" (840 traffic/mo, 92 keywords)
- "cinnamon and honey benefits" (826 traffic/mo, 78 keywords)
- "home remedies for oral thrush in babies" (737 traffic/mo, 196 keywords)
- "natural makeup remover" (583 traffic/mo, 168 keywords)
- "natural hair removal" (562 traffic/mo, 117 keywords)

**The pattern:** Tom's publishes broadly on wellness, beauty, and home topics that their target customer cares about — even when those topics don't directly relate to their product catalog. This is a classic TOF editorial strategy that builds topical authority and brand awareness. Each of these pages ranks for 45–196 keywords, generating compounding traffic.

**What RSC is missing:** RSC publishes almost no content of this type. The brand has no posts on adjacent wellness topics (oral health habits, skin routines, natural home care, ingredient education at scale). Tom's "how to get sweat smell out of clothes" (541 traffic/mo) is a brilliant adjacent topic for a deodorant brand — RSC has no equivalent.

### Dr. Bronner's — Product-Led SEO with Deep Ingredient Education

Dr. Bronner's top pages reveal a dual strategy:

1. **Deep ingredient/educational content:** "castile soap ingredients" (484 traffic/mo, 59 keywords), "laundry soap vs detergent" (596 traffic/mo, 107 keywords), "soap for skin allergy" (512 traffic/mo, 175 keywords), "what soap is safe for cats" (487 traffic/mo, 162 keywords)
2. **High-authority product pages that rank for informational queries:** Their lip balm product page drives 943 traffic/mo across 29 keywords including "organic lip balm" — the product page itself does SEO work

**What RSC is missing:** RSC's product pages appear to do minimal SEO lifting. Dr. Bronner's product and collection pages rank for dozens of keywords each. RSC should be building out its `natural-lip-balm`, `natural-bar-soap`, and `fluoride-free-toothpaste` collection pages with substantial on-page content (ingredient explanations, use cases, FAQs) so they compete for informational queries, not just branded searches.

RSC also has no ingredient-deep-dive content. Posts like "What Is Sodium Lauryl Sulfate and Why Avoid It?" or "Coconut Oil in Skincare: The Full Ingredient Breakdown" would mirror Dr. Bronner's strategy and support RSC's existing SLS/fluoride content cluster.

### OSEA Malibu — Brand Authority and Category Ownership

OSEA's top pages are almost entirely brand-name searches ("osea body butter," "osea body scrub," "osea moisturizer") combined with one breakout educational piece on "how to use hyaluronic acid" (677 traffic/mo, 122 keywords).

**What RSC can learn:** OSEA demonstrates that category ownership at the product level (ranking #1 for brand + category terms) is a viable strategy — but it requires investing in the brand enough that people search for it specifically. RSC's "real skin" keyword at position #6 with 350/mo is a seed of this. OSEA also proves that a single, deeply useful educational piece on a trending ingredient (hyaluronic acid) can drive outsized traffic.

**The gap for RSC:** RSC's coconut oil content is the equivalent opportunity — coconut oil is RSC's "hero ingredient," yet keywords like "coconut oil for skin benefits" (1,000/mo, KD 31, RSC pos. 63) and "organic coconut oil for skin" (800/mo, KD 12, RSC pos. 99) are nearly unreachable from RSC's current content. Consolidating multiple thin coconut oil posts into one authoritative, comprehensive guide would replicate OSEA's hyaluronic acid play.
