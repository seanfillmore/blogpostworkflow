## 3. INGREDIENT ACCURACY
**VERDICT:** BLOCKER — FAILED  
**NOTES:** Multiple critical errors detected:

1. **Xylitol claim (MAJOR):** Post states "Xylitol, a natural sugar alcohol found in plants" and lists it as a key ingredient to look for. **Per product ingredients provided, the formula contains stevia, NOT xylitol.** This is a direct contradiction. The ingredient list shows "stevia" explicitly; xylitol does not appear. This error appears in both the "Key Ingredients to Look for" section and the comparison table.

2. **Cinnamon & clove essential oils omitted from product description:** In the "Best No Fluoride Toothpaste for Adults" subsection, the post describes the toothpaste as containing "peppermint and spearmint" for freshness and clove for antimicrobial benefit. However, the full ingredient list is only listed once, later in the post. The product description should note that **organic essential oil of cinnamon AND clove** are in the formula—both are listed in the canonical ingredients. This is less critical than the xylitol error but still a factual gap.

3. **Hydroxyapatite (nHA) framed as present ingredient:** The post repeatedly lists "Hydroxyapatite (nHA)" as a key ingredient to look for in fluoride-free toothpaste and includes it in the comparison table as an alternative to fluoride. **Per the provided ingredient list, Real Skin Care's toothpaste does NOT contain hydroxyapatite.** The post never explicitly claims RSC uses it, but the section "Key Ingredients to Look for" presents nHA and xylitol as ingredients *in* quality fluoride-free formulas, immediately followed by "Our own coconut oil toothpaste uses baking soda as a key cleaning ingredient" — readers may infer these alternatives are in RSC's formula when they are not.

**IMPACT:** The xylitol error is a direct factual inaccuracy about what's in Real Skin Care's product. This must be corrected before publication. The hydroxyapatite framing is misleading in context.
