## Factual Concerns
VERDICT: Two items to verify or qualify
NOTES:

1. **"Apply within 2–3 minutes after showering"** appears twice — once in the How to Use list (correctly stated as 2–3 minutes) and once in the General Tips section (stated as "2–3 minutes"). However, the *Eczema-prone skin* bullet in the Skin Goal section says *"Apply within 3 minutes of bathing"* — a slight inconsistency. Standardize to "within 2–3 minutes" throughout.

2. **"Coconut oil penetrates the skin barrier to soften rough patches and provide deep hydration."** The claim that coconut oil *penetrates* the skin barrier is commonly repeated but contested in dermatology literature — it primarily functions as an occlusive emollient rather than a true skin-penetrating agent. Consider softening to *"helps soften rough patches and supports hydration by sealing moisture in."*

3. **PFAS claim — source status:** The post cites the FDA's December 2025 PFAS report and links to the EWG article covering it. The EWG link appears functional and plausible. However, per standing instructions, the FDA report itself is listed in References only as a plain-text citation with no URL. If this report exists and is publicly accessible, add a direct link. If the URL cannot be verified, flag this claim as **"unsourced pending link addition"** — the PFAS section is central to the post's argument and deserves a working source.

4. **"The term 'fragrance' on a label can represent any combination of 3,000+ ingredients, many never tested for safety."** The 3,000+ figure is widely cited by EWG and other advocacy groups but originates from industry fragrance databases, not independent safety reviews. This is a defensible claim but should be attributed: add *"according to EWG"* or similar to avoid overstating certainty.
