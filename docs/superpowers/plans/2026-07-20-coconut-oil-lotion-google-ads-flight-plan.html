02 / THE MATH

The number you can’t out-run

At ROAS 2×, allowable cost-per-sale is order value ÷ 2. A $30 lotion gives you a $15 ceiling — and your clicks cost ~$1.34. On cold Search you’d need a ~9% conversion rate to break even. Realistic cold-traffic conversion is 1–3%. So the lotion only works two ways: bigger orders and cheaper, higher-intent clicks.

04 / CONVERSION

Make the lotion actually sell

Same clicks as last time, four things fixed. These are the difference between 0.23% and a rate that pays.

Fix first

Verify conversion tracking before spending a dollar. One tracked sale on 432 clicks, while the store does 1–2 orders/day, means Google is blind — and smart bidding can’t optimize toward a signal it can’t see.

Confirm the Ads purchase conversion fires on the Shopify thank-you page, passes order value + transaction ID, dedupes, and has Enhanced Conversions on. Place one live test order and confirm it records.

Raise AOV

Sell the set, or push the cart over a free-shipping threshold. This is what moves the math from “don’t” to “best” — a $47 order can afford a $23 sale, a $30 order can’t.

Free shipping at ~$45, a lotion + moisturizer bundle, and a one-click cart upsell. Point paid traffic at the set page, not the bare $30 PDP.

Land + offer

Every paid click hits a page built to sell — PDP or set, never a blog post — with a first-order incentive and visible trust.

A welcome offer (e.g. 15% off first order), reviews and ingredient/trust badges above the fold, clear coconut-oil / natural USP. Your 6.6% CTR proves the promise lands; the page has to keep it.

Bank the LTV

Lotion is a consumable — capture the customer and earn the repeat. That’s what makes an “acquire customers” goal add up to ROAS 2× over time, not just on order one.

Email/SMS capture on entry, a subscribe-&-save option, and a post-purchase flow. A near-breakeven first order becomes 2×+ across the second and third.

05 / BUDGET

Prove first, then scale — your rule, made mechanical

“I’ll run higher budgets if the ROAS is 2+” becomes an explicit gate, so money never rides on hope.

07 / SEQUENCE

Launch order

Nothing scales before the thing beneath it is proven. Roughly four weeks to a trustworthy read.

Week 0 · before any spend

Fix the plumbing

Verify conversion tracking end-to-end (live test order). Build the product feed. Stand up the set/bundle, free-ship threshold, first-order offer, and email/SMS capture.

Week 1–2

Launch Brand + Shopping, small

Maximize Clicks. Goal isn’t profit yet — it’s ~15–30 clean conversions and a sanity-checked tracking number.

Week 3–4

Add high-intent Search, switch bidding

If Shopping clears target, turn on the tight Search ad groups to the set/PDP and move winners to Target ROAS.

Ongoing · weekly

Mine, negate, scale

Search-term report → new negatives every week. Apply the 2× gate: scale what earns, pause what doesn’t. Broad prospecting only reopens once the core is profitable.
